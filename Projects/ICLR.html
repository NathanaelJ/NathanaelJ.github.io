The Altitude Record Team at Imperial College London Rocketry

The Altitude Record Team (ART) is a project within Imperial College London Rocketry (ICLR) which exists to break United Kingdom Rocketry Association (UKRA) altitude records. The team formed in 2019, with it's first two launches in early 2021. Three further launches are planned over the 2021-22 academic year.

I lead the aerodynamics and simulations sub-team, using (and writing) computer software to predict the behaviour of our rocket. We make use of packages including OpenRocket, StarCCM+, and recently ESTECO modeFrontier, which has proved to be hugely valuable.

ASTRA

This is a family of test-bed rockets used to verify systems in preparation for record-breaking flights. Two successful flights have taken place, with a further two planned to take place in 2022.

APEX

APEX will be ART's first record attempt, aiming to break the UKRA I-class altitude record. Using a single motor, it will need to fly higher than 2804m (9199ft), travelling at supersonic speeds and calling for a highly-optimised design.
